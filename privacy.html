Snaption – Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 5, 2026

Snaption is a Chrome extension that saves web pages — including their URL, a screenshot, and user-defined metadata — to Notion databases. This policy explains what data the extension handles and how.

Data we collect

Snaption stores the following data locally in your own browser via chrome.storage.sync:

  • Your Notion internal integration token — entered by you in the Options page and used exclusively to authenticate API calls to Notion on your behalf.
  • Your Notion database configurations and templates — the database IDs, property mappings, and display preferences you define.
  • Your theme preference (light / dark / system).

How data is used

  • Your Notion token is sent only to api.notion.com — the official Notion API — as an Authorization header. It is never sent to any server owned or operated by Snaption.
  • Screenshots captured by the extension are uploaded directly to Notion via Notion's file upload API. They do not pass through any intermediary server.
  • No analytics, crash reports, or telemetry are collected.

Data sharing

Snaption does not share, sell, or transmit any of your data to third parties. The only external service the extension communicates with is the Notion API, at your explicit direction (when you press Save).

Data storage

All data is stored in chrome.storage.sync, which is managed by your Chrome/Google account and encrypted by Chrome. Snaption has no access to this data outside of the extension's own context. You can delete all stored data at any time by uninstalling the extension or clearing its storage from Chrome's extension settings.
